VoxT-GNN presents a novel 3D object detection framework utilizing LiDAR point clouds, focusing on challenging scenarios with small and diverse object types. The model integrates two main components: a Voxel-Level Transformer (VoxelFormer) for local feature learning, and a Graph Neural Network Feed-Forward Network (GnnFFN) for global feature aggregation. By employing voxelization and k-nearest neighbors for graph construction, VoxT-GNN enhances the detection of small objects, achieving state-of-the-art performance across datasets like KITTI and Waymo. Experimental results indicate significant improvements in Average Precision (AP) particularly in pedestrian and cyclist detection, with a notable mAP of 65.44% on the KITTI benchmark. Future work aims to optimize the architecture further while maintaining high computational efficiency for real-world applications.
