This paper presents a novel approach called 'Polanyi', aimed at integrating and enhancing large language models (LLMs) for grounded world models (GWMs) that facilitate better understanding and reasoning of complex, real-world scenarios. The method combines strengths of LLMs and structured semantic representations, employing multimodal inputs (e.g., images) to generate knowledge-augmented formal representations of meaning via Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) graphs. The approach incorporates implicit knowledge and utilizes a feedback loop for iterative refinement, enabling the continuous adaptation and enhancement of knowledge graphs. The proposed system is evaluated through a three-tiered framework assessing logical validation, ontology alignment, and human assessment of generated knowledge triples. Results indicate promising performance in enriching knowledge representations across various domains, with potential applications in natural language understanding and complex problem-solving.
