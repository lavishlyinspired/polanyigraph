This paper details a novel ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline for developing a high-quality knowledge graph of Italian legislation using large language models (LLMs) and the Akoma Ntoso XML standard. It emphasizes the advantages of using knowledge graphs over traditional relational databases for representing complex interlinked data, thus facilitating better knowledge discovery. The authors introduce a Property Graph schema that captures the essence of legislative data, allowing for efficient graph analytics. They propose using LLMs, specifically fine-tuned models like BERT and Mistral-7B, to enhance the graph's completeness and accuracy by deriving properties such as domains, titles, and topics from the laws. The performance of the pipeline is evaluated on multiple fronts: accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, and trustworthiness, showing its effectiveness in managing legislative data and enabling advanced analytics.
