The article discusses 'Algorithmic Drift', a novel simulation framework designed to study the long-term effects of recommender systems on user preferences. It introduces metrics to quantify 'algorithmic drift' and highlights that user navigation in social media is influenced by recommendation algorithms. The study addresses concerns about echo chambers and opinion polarization exacerbated by these algorithms and proposes a stochastic model for user interactions with recommendation systems. The framework was validated through extensive evaluations using both synthetic and real-world datasets (e.g., Movielens-1M), showing its effectiveness in capturing user behavior changes over time due to recommendations. The article concludes that the methodology offers a robust tool for understanding the impact of algorithms on user preferences, with implications for ensuring fair and transparent recommendation outcomes.
